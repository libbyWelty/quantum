What is Quantum Computing?

Unlike classical computers that use bits (0s and 1s), quantum computers use qubits. These devices leverage the strange and powerful properties of subatomic particles to perform calculations that would take traditional supercomputers thousands of years to complete.

Key Concepts

Superposition

The ability of a quantum system to be in multiple states at the same time until it is measured.

Entanglement

A phenomenon where two particles become so deeply linked that the state of one instantly influences the state of the other.
